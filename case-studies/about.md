## About Text (Two Columns)

### Column 1
I'm a UX Designer with nearly five years of end-to-end product design experience at Pets at Home, embedded simultaneously across two product teams: one focused on e-commerce conversion for web and app, and the other on ACE, an enterprise Android platform used by over 5,000 store colleagues across 450+ stores. That combination of consumer-facing and enterprise work means I've had to design for very different users, contexts, and constraints, and the depth of systems knowledge and cross-functional relationships I've built over that time means other teams bring me in on product design problems when they don't have a dedicated resource.

### Column 2
My approach is data-driven and pragmatic, which means I use research, analytics, and facilitated discovery to reduce risk and validate direction, but I don't treat process as a performance or reach for workshops and testing unless they'll actually change a decision. I work in agile delivery using Scrum methodology, and I get engineering in the room from discovery onwards because leaving it to handover creates problems that are entirely avoidable.

Recently my focus has been on AI-assisted tooling: using Figma Make and AI tools to produce high-fidelity prototypes that include ARIA tagging and assistive technology support from the start, and building automated research tools that expand what's practical for a single designer to achieve. I tend to learn by doing rather than through formal courses, so the tools I know are the ones I've actually had to use.
